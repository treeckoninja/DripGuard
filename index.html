What this can't tell you

Honesty is the whole point of "stay informed." Heuristics catch habits, not authorship — and the same habits appear in plenty of human writing. Treat a high score as a reason to look closer, never as proof.

It flags style, not authorship

A careful, formal human writer can score high. A lightly edited AI draft can score low. The score reflects how the text reads, not who produced it.

False positives are real

Non-native English writers, technical and academic prose, and anyone taught a tidy transition-word essay structure can trip these signals without touching AI.

It's easy to evade

Anyone gaming a detector can strip these tells out in minutes, so heuristics catch the careless far more than the strategic.

The target keeps moving

As models change and people learn the tells, the patterns shift. A strong signal today may mean little next year.
